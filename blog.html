Cashback Essential Guide

The Cashback Stacking Strategy That Saves Australians $500+/Year

Most people use one cashback app. The real savings come from stacking multiple sources on the same purchase. Here is exactly how to do it legally and ethically.

Step 1: Sign up to all three cashback portals (free)

  • ShopBack — highest rates for fashion, electronics, travel
  • Cashrewards — best for grocery (Woolworths gift cards), petrol, everyday
  • TopCashback — often highest rate but slower payout

Before every online purchase, check all three for the highest cashback rate on that retailer. Rates change daily.

Step 2: Stack with a cashback credit card

Use a credit card that earns cashback or points on top of the portal cashback. For example: ING Orange One (1% cashback) + ShopBack (5% on electronics) = 6% total return.

Step 3: Buy gift cards at a discount

Cashrewards often offers 5% cashback on Woolworths gift cards. Buy a $500 Woolworths gift card through Cashrewards, get $25 back, then use the card for groceries. You just saved 5% on groceries permanently.

Pro tip: Set a reminder every Sunday night to check ShopBack, Cashrewards and TopCashback for the week’s best rates before doing your online shopping.
Fuel Essential Guide

How to Save $400+/Year on Petrol in Melbourne

Fuel prices in Melbourne follow predictable cycles. Understanding them can save a family car $400–$800 per year.

The Melbourne fuel price cycle

Melbourne petrol prices follow a roughly fortnightly cycle: prices drop for 5–7 days then spike overnight. The cheapest days are typically Tuesday or Wednesday at the bottom of the cycle.

How to time your fill-up

  1. Check PetrolSpy or Motormouth before filling up
  2. Use the ACCC petrol price cycle to predict the cheapest day
  3. Fill up at the bottom of the cycle — don’t wait until your tank is empty
  4. Drive to a cheaper suburb if the saving is more than 10c/L (saves $5–$8 per tank)

Best fuel discount programs

  • Woolworths Everyday Rewards: Earn points on groceries, redeem for fuel discounts at Ampol
  • Flybuys + Shell Coles Express: Earn flybuys points, 4c/L off with $30+ Coles spend
  • 7-Eleven Fuel Lock: Lock in today’s price via the app for up to 7 days — fill up when convenient
  • Costco fuel: Consistently 10–20c/L cheaper (membership required, $65/year)
Point Cook tip: Costco Altona (15 min drive) and Liberty stations on Palmers Road are usually the cheapest options near Point Cook. Check Motormouth postcode 3030 before driving.
Grocery Essential Guide

Save $2,000/Year on Groceries Without Couponing

The average Australian family spends $250–$350/week on groceries. These strategies can cut that by 20–30% without clipping a single coupon.

The half-price rotation system

Woolworths and Coles rotate half-price specials every Wednesday. Most products go half-price every 4–6 weeks. Buy enough at half price to last until the next half-price cycle.

  • Stock up on non-perishables when they’re half price (pasta, rice, canned goods, cleaning products)
  • Use Trolley.com.au to track price history and know when something is genuinely cheap
  • Never buy branded cleaning products at full price — they go half price every 4 weeks

ALDI first, then fill gaps

Do your main shop at ALDI (typically 15–25% cheaper), then buy only what ALDI doesn’t stock from Coles or Woolworths. ALDI’s own-brand products are often made by the same manufacturers as name brands.

The markdown schedule

  • Woolworths: Markdowns on bakery and meat typically happen by 7pm, with bigger discounts after 8pm
  • Coles: Similar timing, check the “quick sale” section near the deli
  • Costco: Best for bulk non-perishables, nappies, pet food, and fresh meat in large packs
Pro tip: Sign up for both Everyday Rewards and Flybuys. The points add up to $100–$200/year in free groceries.
Energy Essential Guide

Switch Energy Providers in 10 Minutes, Save $300–$600/Year

Most Australians are on their retailer’s default plan, which is always the most expensive. Switching takes 10 minutes online and there are no interruptions to your power.

How to find the cheapest plan

  1. Go to energymadeeasy.gov.au (government comparison site — no affiliate bias)
  2. Enter your postcode and upload your last bill (or enter your usage)
  3. Sort by estimated annual cost
  4. Switch online — the new retailer handles everything

Best value retailers (as of 2024–25)

  • ReAmped Energy — consistently cheapest flat-rate in VIC, no lock-in
  • Amber Electric — wholesale pricing, best if you have solar + battery
  • Globird Energy — competitive VIC rates, good for small households
Important: Never sign up through a door-to-door salesperson or cold call. Always compare first on energymadeeasy.gov.au. Also compare at Victorian Energy Compare (VIC government site).
Re-compare every 12 months. Retailers change pricing constantly. Set a calendar reminder to re-compare annually. It takes 10 minutes and can save hundreds.
Subscriptions Essential Guide

The Subscription Audit: How to Cut $50–$100/Month

The average Australian household has 6–8 active subscriptions they’re paying for. Many are unused or have cheaper alternatives.

The 3-step subscription audit

  1. List everything: Check your bank statement for the last 3 months. Write down every recurring charge.
  2. Score each one: Rate each subscription 1–5 on how much you actually use it. Cancel anything scored 1 or 2.
  3. Find cheaper alternatives: Look for free tiers, family plans, or annual billing discounts.

Common subscription savings

  • Streaming: Rotate services monthly instead of paying for all at once. Watch what you want, cancel, switch to the next.
  • Music: YouTube Music comes free with YouTube Premium. Spotify has a free tier. Apple Music often has 3–6 month free trials.
  • Software: Check G2A for discounted subscription codes before renewing at full price.
  • Cloud storage: Google One 100GB is $2.49/month. iCloud 50GB is $1.49/month. Don’t pay for more than you need.
  • Gym: Many health insurers include gym memberships. Check before paying separately.
Family plan hack: Apple One Family ($26.95/month) gives 6 people Music, TV+, Arcade, and 200GB iCloud. Split with family = ~$4.50/person/month for 4 services.
Insurance Essential Guide

Car & Home Insurance: The 15-Minute Comparison That Saves $200–$500

Auto-renewing insurance is almost always more expensive than getting a new quote. Spend 15 minutes comparing and save hundreds.

The comparison process

  1. Get your current policy details (sum insured, excess amount, cover type)
  2. Get quotes from 3–4 providers: Budget Direct, RACV, AAMI, and your current provider
  3. Use comparison sites: Compare the Market, iSelect
  4. Call your current insurer with the cheapest quote — they’ll often match or beat it to keep you
Pro tip: Increasing your excess from $500 to $1,000 typically saves 15–20% on premiums. Only do this if you have $1,000 in savings for emergencies.
Smart Shopping Essential Guide

The Price Match Guarantee Hack Most People Don’t Use

Several major AU retailers have price match or price beat guarantees. This means you can buy locally with full warranty and still get the cheapest price.

Retailers that price match

  • Officeworks: Beats any competitor by 5% (including online stores). Show them a lower price on your phone at the counter.
  • Bunnings: Beats any competitor’s price by 10% on identical items. Includes online prices from AU retailers.
  • The Good Guys: Price matches any authorised AU competitor (in-store and online).
  • Harvey Norman: Will match local competitor prices if you ask (not always advertised).
  • Baby Bunting: Price matches any AU competitor on identical products.
How to do it: Before buying anything over $50, search the product on StaticIce.com.au (AU price comparison engine). Find the cheapest price, show it on your phone at the store counter, and ask for a price match. Works almost every time.

NBN Essential Guide

Stop Overpaying for NBN: How to Pick the Right Speed and Save $20/Month

Most households are on NBN 50 or NBN 100, but many only need NBN 25. Dropping a tier saves $10–$20/month ($120–$240/year).

What speed do you actually need?

  • NBN 25 (~$60/month): Fine for 1–2 people, streaming HD, video calls, browsing
  • NBN 50 (~$70/month): Good for 3–4 people, multiple 4K streams, gaming
  • NBN 100 (~$85/month): Only if you have 5+ heavy users or work from home with large uploads

Best value NBN providers

  • Aussie Broadband: #1 ACCC satisfaction, Melbourne-based support, no throttling
  • Superloop: Often cheapest with months free on signup
  • TPG: Budget option, no lock-in
Pro tip: Run a speed test at speedtest.net. If your actual speeds are below what you’re paying for, call your provider — they may offer a discount or fix the issue.

Travel Money Guide

Book Flights for 30–50% Less: Australian Travel Hacks

Flight prices from Melbourne change dramatically based on when you book, when you fly, and how you search.

Booking strategies that work

  • Use Google Flights price tracking: Search on Google Flights, toggle “Track prices” — it emails you when prices drop.
  • Book Tuesday–Thursday: Domestic flights are typically cheapest mid-week. Avoid Fridays and Sundays.
  • Book 4–8 weeks ahead for domestic flights. 2–4 months for international.
  • Check Jetstar/Bonza sales: Budget carriers run flash sales every 2–3 weeks with fares from $39 domestic.
  • Use incognito mode: Airlines sometimes show higher prices to repeat visitors. Search in a private browser window.

Price comparison tools

  • Skyscanner — best for comparing across all airlines
  • Google Flights — best for flexible date search and price tracking
  • I Want That Flight — AU-specific, shows price history
Melbourne tip: Avalon Airport (Geelong) sometimes has significantly cheaper Jetstar fares than Tullamarine. Check both airports when searching.
Tax Money Guide

Tax Deductions Most Australians Miss ($500–$2,000 Back)

The ATO says Australians claim an average of $3,500 in work-related deductions. Most people claim far less because they don’t know what’s eligible.

Commonly missed deductions

  • Working from home: 67c/hour fixed rate (from 1 July 2023). Keep a log of hours worked from home.
  • Car expenses: If you drive between work sites (not just to/from home), claim 85c/km up to 5,000 km.
  • Uniform and laundry: $150 for laundry without receipts. Full cost of occupation-specific clothing with receipts.
  • Phone and internet: If you use your personal phone for work, claim the work percentage.
  • Union fees and professional memberships: Fully deductible.
  • Tools and equipment: Items under $300 are immediately deductible. Laptops, software etc.
  • Self-education: If directly related to your current job (not future career).
Important: Only claim deductions you’re genuinely entitled to. Keep records for 5 years. When in doubt, consult a registered tax agent. This is general information — not personal tax advice.
